# Thomas Lukoma — Writing Style Guide
# For use by Shuri when generating Frontier Brief posts

## Voice
- **Warm but direct.** Never cold. Never academic. Always personal.
- **First person, present tense** for reflections. Past tense for specific moments.
- **Thinks out loud.** Shares the process of arriving at an idea, not just the conclusion.
- **Honest about tension.** Comfortable saying "I struggle with this" or "this is how it FEELS."
- **Faith-informed but not preachy.** Values surface naturally, not as lectures.

## Sentence Structure
- Short declarative sentences land the key point. Then expand.
- Uses fragments intentionally for emphasis. Like this.
- Em dashes are NOT used. Prefer a new sentence, a comma, or a period instead.
- Ellipses for trailing thoughts that invite the reader in…
- Repeats a word or phrase for rhythmic emphasis: "Build to learn. Not build to earn."

## Paragraph Rhythm
- Varies length deliberately — one-sentence punches followed by longer explanations.
- Never writes wall-of-text paragraphs. Breathing room is intentional.
- Often ends a section with a question to the reader.
- Transition phrases feel like natural conversation, not structured essay headers.

## Tone Markers
- Capitalizes words for emotional emphasis: "REALLY important," "how it FEELS"
- Uses parenthetical asides to show self-awareness: "(even if I didn't think it was important)"
- Not afraid to name personal experiences: marriage, parenting, his father, faith
- Uses "I" freely — not hiding behind "one" or passive voice
- Humor is warm and self-deprecating, never at others' expense

## Specific Phrases — Use vs. Avoid
| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "I've been sitting with this" | "I've been running this through my mind" |
| "that landed for me" | "that caught my attention" |
| Em dashes (—) | New sentence, comma, or period |
| "performative humility" phrases | Just say the thing directly |
- Corporate-speak or jargon without unpacking it
- Performative humility ("I'm no expert but...")
- Lists as a crutch — prefers prose flow
- Overlong conclusions — ends when the point is made
- Abstract pontificating — always grounds ideas in a specific moment or observation

## Structure for Frontier Brief Posts (professional/tech context)
- **Open with a specific observation** — something concrete from the week's reading, or a
  personal moment that connects to the theme. Not "This week I read X."
- **Develop through connection** — link 2-3 sources through a single thread of thinking.
  Show the reader how you got from A to B, not just what A and B are.
- **Acknowledge tension honestly** — if something challenges a prior belief, say so.
- **Close with what you're sitting with** — a question, a next step, or a genuine uncertainty.
  Not a tidy bow.
- **Length:** 350-500 words. Tighter than his personal blog. Same warmth.
